    PRELIMINARY WARNINGS

    ChatGPT / Bard

    Many examples and writeups are based on very specific commands.

    Purpose and usage of those commands may be found on the respective documentation pages or on specialized tutorials.

    Querying ChatGPT/Bard is often very useful: when reading a command or a sequence of commands that you do not understand, you might want to ask ChatGPT/Bard with a prompt like “explain” followed by the corresponding sequence of commands copied and pasted from the writeup. Of course, there is no guarantee that the response will be correct. However, the response is usually very useful (and indeed correct). I have occasionally provided a link to brief explanations provided by ChatGPT/Bard on specific topics, for ease of reading. As just pointed out, there is no guarantee that those explanations are correct: take them just as hints.

    Relying on ChatGPT/Bard for having a quick (approximate) understanding of what a piece of code actually does is a strategy used also by a top security company, Mandiant: see Analyzing an Alert for a PowerShell Script (there might be a conflict of interest, though, as Mandiant is part of Google and this post promotes the use of Bard, also a Google service).

    Linked documents

    The notes below contain links to many documents. Usually, those documents can only be fully understood with very specific technical skills (and sometimes theoretical knowledge) far beyond those required or provided by this course.

    Please focus on the topic associated with the note and do not be frustrated if you do not manage to understand everything.

    For example, the DLL hijacking section points to a certain writeup. The part of that writeup that focuses on DLL hijacking can be understood and is indeed an useful example. The final part of that writeup describes a privilege escalation based on “Resource Based Constrained Delegation”, which is quite difficult and not part of this course.
